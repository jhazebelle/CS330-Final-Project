# CS330-Final-Project

How do I approach designing software?

When I design software, I like to start with a clear vision of what I want the final product to look like before I begin writing code. For this project, I chose to recreate a Japanese style bento lunch because it had several recognizable objects that could be broken down into simple geometric shapes. I found that planning the scene first made it much easier to decide what meshes, textures, and lighting I would need. One of the biggest design skills I developed during this project was learning how to simplify complex objects into combinations of basic shapes while still making them recognizable. Throughout the milestones, I followed an iterative design process by first creating the basic objects, then positioning them correctly, adding textures, applying lighting, and finally making visual improvements like the lettuce backdrop. This approach helped me avoid getting too overwhelmed and is something I can apply to future software projects by building a solid foundation before focusing on small details.

How do I approach developing programs?

This project also changed how I approach developing software. Instead of trying to make everything perfect on the first attempt, I learned to build one feature at a time, test it, and then move on to the next improvement. Every milestone built on the previous one, which taught me how valuable incremental development can be. I also became much more comfortable debugging problems, reading OpenGL code, and making small changes until I achieved the result I wanted. By the end of the project, I was much more confident working with transformations, textures, lighting, shaders, and camera controls than I was at the beginning of the course. I also learned the importance of commenting my code and organizing it into logical sections, which made debugging and adding new features much easier.

How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science plays a major role in helping me reach my long term goals because I eventually want to build software products and start my own technology company. This project gave me a much better understanding of computational graphics, rendering, lighting, textures, and camera systems, which are concepts used in many modern applications, simulations, and games. While I may not directly build 3D graphics every day as a software engineer, the problem solving skills I developed throughout this course will carry over into many areas of software development. Professionally, these skills have strengthened my programming experience with C++, OpenGL, debugging, and software design. Educationally, they have given me more confidence to continue learning advanced graphics, game development, visualization, and other areas of computer science as I continue working toward my bachelor's degree and future software engineering career.
